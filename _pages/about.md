Hi, my name is Sam. I am a PhD student at [UC Irvine](https://uci.edu/) in the [Swarup Lab](https://swaruplab.bio.uci.edu/), whose work is centered around studying neurodegenerative disorders such as Alzheimer's Disease (AD) using high-throughput genomic methods. With the guidance of my thesis advisor [Vivek Swarup](https://www.faculty.uci.edu/profile.cfm?faculty_id=6444), my research is focused on integrative analysis of multi-omics data of the diseased brain in primary human samples and model systems, using analytical approaches such as statistical modeling, network analysis, and deep-learning.

I am broadly interested in neuroscience, disease biology, molecular genetics, bioinformatics, data science, and machine learning. Combined with my undergraduate training in [Bioengineering & Bioinformatics](http://bioengineering.ucsd.edu/bioinformatics) at UC San Diego, my graduate program in Mathematical Computational Systems Biology [(MCSB)](https://ccbs.uci.edu/education/mcsb/) has allowed me to develop a multi-disciplinary skillset suitable for tackling the most pressing challenges in biology.

Outside of the lab, I enjoy spending time exploring the natural beauty and vibrant culture of Southern California. You can check out some of my adventures on this website! Additionally, I enjoy spending time with my friends playing board games, role-playing games, and video games, some of which you can check out on my twitter feed.
